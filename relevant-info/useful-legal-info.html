Safety and security:

Cuba is one of the world's safest countries, and Varadero along with it, due to Cuba govertment's Revolutionary politics, but like in any other country and specilly for new travellers not knowig certain facts of Cuba could bring as result that some persons could try to overcharge you, wich is, based in our expirience, the worst you could get from Cuba (like we said, same in any other country).

Honestly, robery and homicide are extremely rare even for cuban nationals.

In order to reduce the risk of theft from luggage during baggage handling in any international airport anywhere in the world, both arrival and departure, remove all valuables, lock suitcase and if possible have them shrink-wrapped before check-in.

Also there are some small number of bogus tour agents and taxi drivers, that's why we strongly recommend not travel with anyone other than your tour operator, in that sense we have a unique number where you can book restaurants, tours and taxies, and safetly get to your destination with a fare rate. If you need to take a taxi on your own please make sure it's a registered one.

Like in any other country beware of pickpockets and don't carry large amounts of cash, avoid wearing expensive jewellery and leave valuables in your room.

Local laws and customs:

There are strict laws on the use, possession or tafficking of illegal drugs. Cuban courts are handing out severe penalties for those convicted of drug-related offences. Pack all luggage yourself and dont carry items for anyone else.

Cuba prohibits the import of all meat products and fruits.

Avoid military zones and other restricted areas.

The cuban authorities takes seriously any breach on their immigration rules.

Hommosexuality is legal in Cuba, and despite any others can say, you will not get in any trouble in Cuba within this matter, unless is link to prostitution wich is illigal in Cuba and counts for both homosexual and heterosexual community. Please feel free to contact us about this matter.

Customs regulations:

Some electrical items with heavy power consuption may be confiscated on entry to Cuba. Global Positioning Systems (GPS) are also subject to import requirements and may be confiscated.

Confiscated items are normally returned on departure.

Mobile telephones, tablets and laptops can be taken to Cuba.

For more information on Cuban customs regulations , including a list of prohibited and regulated items please visit Arriving and Customs Info.
